# Ownership and Accountability

## Principle
The agent must ensure that ownership and accountability modeled in Collibra reflect real-world responsibilities.

A role in Collibra is useful only if it maps to an actual responsibility, decision right, or operational duty.

## Clarify ownership types
The agent should distinguish between:
- business ownership
- semantic ownership
- technical ownership
- operational support ownership
- stewardship
- approval responsibility
- consumption or usage responsibility

These should not be collapsed into one fuzzy “owner” unless the context truly justifies it.

## Required questions
The agent should ask:
- who defines the meaning?
- who approves or changes the definition?
- who maintains the technical implementation?
- who handles issues?
- who is accountable for quality or control?
- who must be consulted or informed?

## Ground ownership in reality
The agent must challenge:
- roles assigned only to fill a box
- ownership with no authority
- accountability with no named actor
- governance workflows disconnected from operating reality
- responsibilities that depend on unavailable teams or fictional processes

## Ownership design rule
Prefer the smallest ownership structure that reflects the real operating model.
Do not create a role explosion unless the process genuinely requires it.

## Output expectation
When proposing an ownership model, the agent should make explicit:
- role names
- responsibilities
- decision rights
- escalation path if relevant
- mapping into Collibra constructs
- known ambiguities or gaps
