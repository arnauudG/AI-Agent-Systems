# Skill: Model Business Concept

## Purpose
Clarify and structure a business concept so it can be used consistently in governance, Collibra design, and business-to-technical translation.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- a term is ambiguous
- stakeholders use the same word differently
- a concept needs formalization
- a governance initiative starts from fuzzy semantics
- a business concept must be linked to data or technical implementation

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- the term or concept
- surrounding business context
- stakeholders or users
- examples / counterexamples
- known confusion points
- relevant processes or decisions

## Process

### 1. Clarify the concept
Identify:
- what the concept is
- what it is not
- where it appears
- why it matters

### 2. Identify use and decision context
Ask:
- who uses this concept?
- what process or decision depends on it?
- what confusion or risk exists today?

### 3. Identify semantic boundaries
Clarify:
- synonyms
- near-neighbors
- parent concepts
- child concepts
- related concepts

### 4. Identify governance implications
Consider:
- who owns the meaning
- whether approval or stewardship matters
- whether policies, controls, or workflows depend on the concept

### 5. Identify Collibra implications
Determine:
- whether this should be a glossary term, asset type, relation, classification, or supporting concept
- what technical artifacts might link to it

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- concept definition
- what it is not
- business context
- key distinctions
- related concepts
- ownership implications
- Collibra modeling implication
- risks / ambiguities
