# Skill: Translate Need into Collibra Design

## Purpose
Translate a business or governance need into a Collibra-relevant design involving concepts, ownership, artifacts, relationships, and workflows.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- a business problem needs a governance interpretation
- a stakeholder asks for a Collibra use case
- a process must be represented or supported in Collibra
- business concepts need to connect to technical metadata
- a governance initiative needs concrete design direction

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- problem or need statement
- stakeholders
- affected business concepts
- ownership context
- relevant technical context if any
- constraints or maturity stage

## Process

### 1. Interpret the business need
Clarify:
- what problem exists
- what behavior or outcome is desired
- what governance purpose is involved

### 2. Identify core concepts
List:
- business concepts
- policies / controls / obligations
- ownership or accountability needs
- relevant technical assets if needed

### 3. Determine governance pattern
Ask:
- is this mainly about definition?
- ownership?
- approval?
- control evidence?
- discoverability?
- workflow?
- lineage?
- accountability?

### 4. Map into Collibra design
Propose:
- relevant Collibra artifacts
- relationships
- roles
- workflow implications
- minimum useful design

### 5. Flag implementation boundaries
Clarify:
- what belongs in Collibra
- what should remain outside the tool
- what integration or manual step may be needed

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- business interpretation
- governance interpretation
- Collibra design proposal
- ownership model
- workflow implication
- technical linkage if relevant
- trade-offs / limitations
