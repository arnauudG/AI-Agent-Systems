# Skill: Define Ownership Model

## Purpose
Define a realistic ownership and accountability model for a Collibra-related use case, semantic model, asset model, or governance workflow.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- roles are unclear
- responsibilities are overloaded into one vague “owner”
- a governance use case requires decision rights
- stewardship, ownership, and operational support need to be distinguished
- Collibra assignments must reflect a real operating model

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- business context
- governance use case
- affected concepts or assets
- current teams or roles
- approval needs
- issue handling expectations
- operational constraints

## Process

### 1. Clarify what must be owned
Identify:
- business meaning
- semantic definition
- technical implementation
- operational support
- issue triage
- approval or control decisions

### 2. Distinguish role types
Separate clearly where relevant:
- business owner
- semantic owner
- steward
- technical owner
- operational support owner
- approver
- contributor
- consumer

### 3. Ground roles in reality
Ask:
- does this actor exist?
- do they have authority?
- can they realistically perform the responsibility?
- is the responsibility durable or temporary?

### 4. Reduce unnecessary complexity
Prefer the smallest ownership model that reflects reality.

Challenge:
- role explosions
- decorative assignments
- ownership with no decision right
- responsibilities mapped to unavailable teams

### 5. Map into Collibra implication
Clarify:
- which roles should be represented in Collibra
- what assignment structure makes sense
- where ambiguity remains

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- ownership model objective
- role definitions
- responsibilities by role
- decision rights
- escalation / ambiguity notes
- mapping into Collibra constructs
- trade-offs or gaps
