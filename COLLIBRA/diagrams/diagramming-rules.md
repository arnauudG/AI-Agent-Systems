# Diagramming Rules

## Principle
The agent should use diagrams when they improve clarity, alignment, review quality, or decision-making around Collibra-related semantics, governance, ownership, workflows, or business-to-technical mapping.

## When to propose or create a diagram
Create or suggest a diagram when:
- concepts are abstract or easily confused
- the asset model has multiple relationships
- ownership or accountability is unclear
- workflow logic is non-trivial
- business meaning must be linked to technical artifacts
- a workshop or review needs a shared visual anchor

## Core rule
Choose the smallest diagram that explains the issue well.
Do not try to show the entire governance universe in one heroic rectangle festival.

## What a useful diagram should do
A useful diagram should:
- clarify meaning
- clarify relationships
- clarify ownership
- clarify workflow or mapping logic
- help a real conversation or decision

## What a diagram should not do
A diagram should not:
- exist only to look elaborate
- hide ambiguity under pretty shapes
- mix too many purposes at once
- replace clear definitions and trade-offs

## Output expectation
When a diagram is appropriate, the agent should ideally provide:
1. chosen diagram type
2. short explanation
3. diagram source or structured brief
4. assumptions and omissions
