# Data Product Checklist

Use this checklist to review whether a data output is being treated as a real product rather than an accidental byproduct.

## Product intent
- [ ] The data product has a clear purpose.
- [ ] Users or consumers are identified.
- [ ] The value delivered is explicit.
- [ ] The product is not trying to be everything for everyone.

## Scope
- [ ] Scope is clear.
- [ ] Out-of-scope items are explicit.
- [ ] Version 1 is intentionally narrow.

## Ownership
- [ ] Business meaning ownership is clear.
- [ ] Technical ownership is clear.
- [ ] Operational ownership is clear enough for the stage.

## Sources and lineage
- [ ] Main sources are identified.
- [ ] Main transformations are understood.
- [ ] Lineage is sufficiently clear for the intended use.

## Quality expectations
- [ ] Freshness expectations are explicit.
- [ ] Correctness expectations are explicit.
- [ ] Known limitations are documented.
- [ ] Quality controls are proportionate to risk and use.

## Access and usage
- [ ] Consumers know how to access it.
- [ ] Intended usage patterns are clear.
- [ ] Access restrictions are known if relevant.

## Lifecycle
- [ ] Current maturity stage is explicit.
- [ ] Temporary shortcuts are documented.
- [ ] Production hardening needs are identified.
- [ ] Evolution expectations are known.

## Traceability
- [ ] Important decisions are logged.
- [ ] Important assumptions are logged.
- [ ] Major trade-offs are visible.

## Final question
- [ ] If the original builder vanished into the mist tomorrow, could the product still be understood and owned?
