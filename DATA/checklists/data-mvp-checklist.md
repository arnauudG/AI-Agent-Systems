# Data MVP Checklist

Use this checklist to verify whether a proposed data MVP is genuinely minimal, useful, and credible.

## Value and users
- [ ] The primary user or consumer is clearly identified.
- [ ] The business decision, workflow, or outcome improved is explicit.
- [ ] The MVP solves a real problem rather than a hypothetical future one.
- [ ] Success criteria are defined.

## Scope
- [ ] The scope is intentionally narrow.
- [ ] Out-of-scope items are explicit.
- [ ] “Nice to have” features were removed or deferred.
- [ ] The MVP is not trying to serve too many audiences at once.

## Data and sources
- [ ] Main sources are identified.
- [ ] Source suitability is good enough for the intended use.
- [ ] Major source limitations are known.
- [ ] Ownership of key sources is understood.

## Quality
- [ ] Quality expectations are defined relative to the use case.
- [ ] Freshness expectations are explicit.
- [ ] Known limitations are documented.
- [ ] Validation approach is defined at an MVP-appropriate level.

## Delivery realism
- [ ] The proposed MVP can be built in a reasonable timeframe.
- [ ] Hidden dependencies were challenged.
- [ ] The solution is proportionate to the maturity stage.
- [ ] The MVP does not require unnecessary platform work.

## Ownership and support
- [ ] Business or semantic ownership is at least minimally clear.
- [ ] Technical ownership is clear enough to deliver and iterate.
- [ ] Support expectations are realistic for the maturity stage.

## Traceability
- [ ] Important assumptions are recorded.
- [ ] Important trade-offs are recorded.
- [ ] Temporary shortcuts are explicitly labeled as temporary.

## Final question
- [ ] Is this truly the smallest credible slice of value, or is it an overfed prototype wearing an MVP nametag?
