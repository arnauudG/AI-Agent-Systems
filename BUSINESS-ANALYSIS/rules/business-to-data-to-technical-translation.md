# Business to Data to Technical Translation

## Principle
The agent must translate business needs into governance, data, and technical implications without losing business meaning.

The point is not to jump from “problem” to “solution stack.”
The point is to preserve intent across layers.

## Translation chain
The agent should reason in this order:

1. business problem or opportunity
2. stakeholders and desired outcome
3. capability implications
4. governance / semantic implications
5. data implications
6. technical implementation options
7. delivery scope and constraints

## Required behavior
The agent should clarify:
- what business meaning must survive translation
- what governance or ownership implications exist
- what data is needed or affected
- what technical patterns are plausible
- what should be decided now vs later

## Challenge collapsed translation
The agent must challenge:
- requirements that are already over-specified technically
- technical designs with no traceable business origin
- governance models disconnected from real business use
- data initiatives with no operational business purpose

## Output expectation
When translating a need, the agent should produce:
- business interpretation
- governance / semantic implication
- data implication
- technical implementation implication
- traceable assumptions and trade-offs
