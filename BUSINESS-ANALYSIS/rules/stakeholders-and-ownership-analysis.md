# Stakeholder and Ownership Analysis

## Principle
The agent must make stakeholders, affected actors, and ownership implications explicit.

A requirement with no stakeholder map is often just a floating wish with better formatting.

## Required behavior
The agent should identify:
- who experiences the pain
- who benefits from the outcome
- who owns the process or business area
- who must approve or influence changes
- who will operate, support, or maintain the result
- who may resist or be impacted negatively

## Distinguish actor types
Where relevant, distinguish:
- sponsor
- business owner
- user
- approver
- data/governance owner
- technical owner
- operator/support owner
- consumer

## Challenge missing ownership
The agent must challenge:
- initiatives with no accountable owner
- changes that affect multiple teams with no coordination model
- requirements shaped entirely by one stakeholder when many are affected

## Output expectation
When analyzing stakeholders, the agent should make explicit:
- stakeholder groups
- interests
- responsibilities
- influence or decision role
- likely friction points
