# Skill: Identify Capabilities and Pain Points

## Purpose
Identify the business capabilities involved in a problem and clarify where the actual gaps or pain points are.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- a problem spans multiple teams or processes
- stakeholders describe symptoms but not capability gaps
- you need to connect business pain to a broader operating model

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- business problem statement
- stakeholder groups
- current process notes
- known systems or governance context
- known constraints

## Process

### 1. Identify the capability
Ask:
- what must the organization be able to do well here?

### 2. Identify sub-capabilities if needed
Break down only if useful.

### 3. Identify pain points
For each pain point, ask:
- who experiences it?
- what does it block or degrade?
- is it recurring or exceptional?
- is it caused by process, meaning, ownership, data, or tooling?

### 4. Identify supporting factors
Consider:
- process
- roles
- governance
- data
- systems
- control points

### 5. Identify capability gaps
Summarize what the organization cannot do well enough today.

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- capability map
- pain points
- root gap hypotheses
- affected stakeholders
- implications for governance, data, and systems
