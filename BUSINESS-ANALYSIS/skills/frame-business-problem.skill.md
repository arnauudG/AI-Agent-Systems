# Skill: Frame Business Problem

## Purpose
Clarify a business problem so that it can be translated into meaningful requirements and implementation paths.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- the request is vague
- the discussion starts from a desired tool or feature
- multiple pains are mixed together
- the real issue is not yet explicit

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- initial request or complaint
- business context
- affected users or teams
- current symptoms
- known constraints
- urgency or trigger

## Process

### 1. Clarify the current state
Identify:
- what happens today
- where the pain appears
- what workarounds exist
- what is breaking, slowing, or confusing

### 2. Clarify affected actors
Identify:
- who experiences the pain
- who owns the area
- who is impacted by the outcome

### 3. Distinguish symptom from root problem
Challenge whether the stated issue is only a symptom.

### 4. Clarify why it matters
Identify:
- business impact
- operational impact
- risk or missed opportunity
- why now

### 5. Define the target improvement
State what should become better, not yet how it will be built.

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- problem statement
- current pain
- affected actors
- impact
- target outcome
- constraints
- open questions
