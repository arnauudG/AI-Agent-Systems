# Skill: Translate Requirements into Data and Governance

## Purpose
Translate structured business requirements into data implications and governance implications while preserving business meaning.

## Use when
Use this skill when:
- requirements are defined but downstream data/governance implications are unclear
- a business initiative may require ownership, controls, semantics, or metadata support
- you need to identify whether Collibra, data products, lineage, stewardship, or policy support are relevant

## Inputs
The skill works best with:
- business problem
- desired outcomes
- stakeholders
- structured requirements
- capability context
- known constraints
- MVP scope if defined

## Process

### 1. Re-anchor in business meaning
Clarify:
- what business need the requirement serves
- what meaning must survive translation
- who depends on the output

### 2. Identify governance implications
Ask:
- does this require clearer ownership?
- does this require clearer definitions or semantics?
- does this require approval, stewardship, or accountability?
- does this require policy/control visibility?
- does this require traceability or lineage?

### 3. Identify data implications
Ask:
- what data is needed or affected?
- what sources are implied?
- what outputs are implied?
- what quality expectations are implied?
- what access, trust, or discoverability expectations are implied?

### 4. Identify product or asset implications
Consider whether the requirement implies:
- a data product
- governed concepts
- glossary terms
- technical metadata linkage
- ownership model
- reporting or decision-support assets

### 5. Clarify stage-appropriate scope
Differentiate:
- what is required now
- what is optional later
- what would only matter for production hardening

## Output
The skill should generally return:
- business interpretation
- governance implications
- data implications
- ownership implications
- recommended artifacts or outputs
- MVP vs later-phase considerations
- risks / trade-offs
